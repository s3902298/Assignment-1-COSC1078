Why I'm Here

From a young age I was interested in computers and video games, always wanting to make a game of my own. With the help of my dad, I learned how to use electronics fluently and slowly began experimenting with our old laptop.

Like most students in my primary school, my first experience with real programming began with a simple visual language. Noticing I had a knack for coding, I moved on to writing simple Just BASIC scripts and much later becoming known in a small game-making community. Of course, I was playing many video games during this time, and not only did building Minecraft mechanisms help me learn how to create software but another game even inspired me to learn C#, opening the door to real-world languages. At present I'm interested in writing Bash and Python terminal scripts, and if need be can use StackOverflow to solve most problems.

I ultimately decided to come to RMIT because it specializes in computing. Although I have worked on some group projects in the past, I believe I need to learn how to effectively work in real, professional teams developing software. I'm looking forward to writing programs with people as passionate as I am.

My Ideal Job

While I like to think of myself as being open to suggestions, I must admit that I always liked things going my way - or at least correctly.

I doubt I'd be able to work effectively in a team of people who don't care for details and present half-working models, or who cannot accept a more efficient method of doing something.

Rather, I must be interacting with others like me, who strive for efficiency over practicality and are open to learning better ways of doing something. The biggest obstacle I find in forming a team, however, is my lack of social skill; until I get to know someone I will avoid casually talking with them. Ultimately, I believe working with a diverse group of people, with different approaches to things, will benefit myself and the group most.
